SCIENCE ICONS

Isaac Newton
About

Sir Isaac Newton (1642 – 1727) was an English physicist and mathematician, who more than anyone else, led the world towards scientific revolution. His book Principia is regarded by many as the most important scientific work.

Achievements
  • NEWTON’S THREE LAWS OF MOTION LAID THE FOUNDATION OF CLASSICAL MECHANICS
  • HE WAS THE FIRST TO FORMULATE THE NOTION OF GRAVITY AS A UNIVERSAL FORCE
  • NEWTON’S PRINCIPIA IS ONE OF THE MOST IMPORTANT WORKS IN THE HISTORY OF SCIENCE
  • HE DISCOVERED THE GENERALISED BINOMIAL THEOREM IN 1665
  • ISAAC NEWTON INVENTED CALCULUS
  • HE INVENTED THE FIRST REFLECTING TELESCOPE
  • HE DID GROUND BREAKING WORK IN OPTICS
  • HE IDENTIFIED LIGHT AS THE SOURCE OF COLOUR SENSATION
  • HE INFERRED CORRECTLY THE OBLATENESS OF EARTH’S SPHEROIDAL FIGURE
  • Became father of modern science
    • Albert Einstein
    About

    Born in Ulm, Württemberg, Germany in 1879, Albert Einstein had a passion for inquiry that eventually led him to develop the special and general theories of relativity. In 1921, he won the Nobel Prize for physics.

    Achievements
  • 1879 Born to Hermann Einstein (a featherbed salesman) and his wife Pauline in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1884 Receives his first compass around this time, inspiring a lifelong quest to investigate mysteries of the natural world.
  • 889 Settles into a program of self-education at age 10 and begins reading as much about science as he can.
  • 1900 Graduates from the ETH.
  • 1905 Publishes, at age 26, five groundbreaking papers, making this his "annus mirabilis," or miracle year. One of the papers introduces his special theory of relativity and another E = mc2.
  • 1906 Continues working as an examiner at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.
  • 1907 Begins applying the laws of gravity to his special theory of relativity.
  • 1913 Works on his new theory of gravity.
  • 1922 Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for 1921.
  • 1927 Attends fifth Solvay Conference and begins developing the foundation of quantum mechanics with Niels Bohr.
    • Michael Faraday
    About

    Born in 1791 in Newington Butts, England. At the age of 14 he started an apprenticeship at a local bookseller in Blandford Street, where over a 7-year period he read as many books and developed an interest in science.

    Achievements
  • 1821: Discovery of Electromagnetic Rotation
  • 1823: Gas Liquefaction and Refrigeration
  • 1825: Discovery of Benzene
  • 1831: Discovery of Electromagnetic Induction
  • 1834: Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis
  • 1836: Invention of the Faraday Cage
  • 1838 Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences
  • 1845: Discovery of the Faraday Effect – a magneto-optical effect
  • 1845: Discovery of Diamagnetism as a Property of all Matter
  • 1849 Member Royal Institute of the Netherlands
